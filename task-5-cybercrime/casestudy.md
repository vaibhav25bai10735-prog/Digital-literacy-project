# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

## Overview

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate
organizations to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information
such as passwords, banking details, or personal data.

## Scenario

A college student receives an email that appears to be from their
university's IT department, claiming that their account will be
suspended unless they verify their login details. The email includes a
link to a fake website that looks identical to the official university
portal.

## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)

1.  The attacker sends a convincing fake email to the victim.
2.  The victim clicks the link provided in the email.
3.  The victim is redirected to a fraudulent website.
4.  The victim enters their login credentials.
5.  The attacker captures the information and gains unauthorized access.

## Target

Phishing attacks often target students, employees, and individuals who
may not recognize fake emails, especially those who frequently use
online services.

## Consequences

The attacker can access personal accounts, steal sensitive data, and
potentially commit financial fraud. Victims may lose money, face
identity theft, or experience academic or professional disruption.

## Prevention

Users should verify email sources, avoid clicking suspicious links,
enable two-factor authentication, and report phishing attempts to their
institution.
